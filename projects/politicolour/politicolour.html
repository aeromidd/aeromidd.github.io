A data visual showing, for each country in the world,
the predominant branding colour of the most nationally popular political party considered* Left of center and Right of center (in 2021).

I created this as I've noticed in passing that in some countries, there is a tradition or trend in colour branding used by major political parties depending on where they are on the political "left/right" spectrum, while in others, there is a totally different convention. Particularly, the USA stood out to me, where red is typically used to depict Republicans ("right wing") and blue to depict Democrats ("left wing") -- whereas in many places in Europe, the exact opposite is true: red is often reserved by left wing and/or socialist movements and their derivatives, while blue is more likely to be used as a color denoting conservative/right wing parties. Based on these results, it appears that this trend holds in many regions across the world (with the US being a bit of an odd-one-out), but there are some other interesting exceptions I didn't know about either.

Data Source: entries semi-automatically searched by
*https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_[SovereignNation]

The predominant branding colour was determined by the colour highlighted in the corresponding party's wikipedia article (the vast majority of parties I selected for here had these labels easily accessible in the right-hand panel and sometimes an RGB value I could take.

If this information was not found by my program, it attempted to discern the most prevalent colour used in the party logo if available, with a manual check to find online photos of rallies and party conferences involving banners, logos, and other branding.
