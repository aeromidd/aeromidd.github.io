Did you know that the Earth and the Moon's size relationship is incredibly unique*?
The mean radius of the Earth in ratio to that of the Moon is about 3:11.
This remarkably means that if you create a square of a side length equivalent to Earth's diameter,
it will have the almost the exact same perimeter length as a circle of diameter equivalent to the Earth and Moons' diameters lined up together.

A neat little fact about the solar system demonstrated in the above animation.

*To date, we don't know of any other planet-moon system with such an exact relationship. Similarly, as far as we have observed, the fact that the sizes of the Moon and the Sun and their relative distances line up so they appear nearly exactly the same size in the sky (allowing the famous corona ring to be observed during solar eclipse events), also appears to be unique to our planet. Wild.
