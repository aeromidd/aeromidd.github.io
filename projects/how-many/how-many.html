What is this graph?

This is an attempt to present a sort of histogram displaying responses to the "how many is a couple/few/several" questions - but instead of just a basic bar chart, this also illustrates the popularity of specific ranges of values or specific exact values.

How do I read it?

If, say, the height of the bars at number 3 totals to 60%, this would mean that the number 3 was present in 60% of all responses to the question, whether that be as an exact value answer OR within a range answer. But the breakdown of stacked blocks under that column will give a more in-depth view of whether most of that 60% of respondents made their answer the exact value of '3', or a range such as '2-4'. The range responses are grouped into unique blocks and may overlap across different heights at other integer values, making it look a bit like a stack of pancakes flopped on top of each other.

To my (limited) knowledge, I'm not aware of such a type of graph already exists.

I'd love to christen it "the Pancake Graph" but unfortunately that's already the name of a different kind of plot.
